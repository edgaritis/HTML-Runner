3-line at thumb is the danger zone

What to judge. At 2 lines the title sits comfortably and the list still has breathing room. At 3 lines the title eats more vertical real estate — list compresses, closer line crowds the brand mark. This is where the engine's auto-shrink will kick in: if total content exceeds safe-zone, the title scales down. For this static test no shrink is applied — you're seeing what 76px does naturally at each length so we can set the auto-shrink floor (probably never below 60px).

Bait point check. The 1-line and 2-line cards both contain the original "market in the middle of a mountain" item at #3. At thumb scale, does it disappear into the list as planned, or does the visual word "market" pull attention from a passing reader?
